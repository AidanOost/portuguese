How questions work

the mechanics

Yes/no questions need no reshuffling — just lift your voice at the end.

É possível?it is possible?Is it possible?

A question word goes straight to the front, then the verb. There’s no English-style “do you…” scaffolding to build.

The é que booster: drop é que (“is it that”) in after the question word. It adds nothing to the meaning but sounds completely natural — you’ll hear it everywhere.

O que é que tem? = O que tem?the what is it that you have?What do you have?

The question words

one card each
o quewhat

Stands on its own. Often padded to o que é que.

O que é que tem?the what is it that you have?What do you have?
O que quer dizer isto?the what means this?What does this mean?
quewhat / which

Used in front of a noun.

Que preferência tem?What preference do you have?
Que tipo de música?What kind of music?
quemwho / whom
Quem pode fazê-lo?Who can do it?
Com quem?With whom?
ondewhere
Onde fica a casa?where is-situated the house?Where is the house?
quandowhen
Quando tem de sair?When do you have to leave?
porquewhy

In this course, “why” takes porque (usually porque é que). Your teacher may write it porquê.

Porque é que não é aceitável?for what is it that not it is acceptable?Why isn’t it acceptable?
comohow
Como está?How are you?
Como se diz…?How do you say…?
quantohow much
Quanto é?How much is it?
Quanto tempo?how much time?How long?
quantos / quantashow many

Agrees with the thing counted — quantos (m), quantas (f).

Quantos dias fica aqui?How many days are you staying?
Quantas horas?How many hours?
a que horasat what time
A que horas te levantas?What time do you get up?
A que horas?at what hours?At what time?
por quewhich (+ noun)

Literally “for what” — picks one out of a set.

Por que restaurante tem preferência?for what restaurant do you have preference?Which restaurant do you prefer?

Easy mix-ups

watch these

porque, two jobs

Same spelling, opposite roles — intonation and context tell them apart.

askPorque é que não vem?Why aren’t you coming?
answerPorque estou cansado.Because I’m tired.

If your teacher writes them, “why” often appears as porquê and “because” stays porque.

o que vs que

Both are “what”, but they stand in different places.

o queO que é que quer?What do you want? (alone)
queQue preferência tem?What preference? (before a noun)

quanto vs quantos

Mass vs count.

quantoQuanto é?How much is it?
quantosQuantos dias?How many days?

quantos / quantas change to match the gender of what you’re counting.

Tip: almost any of these can take the é que booster — Quem é que…?, Onde é que…?, Quando é que…? — without changing the meaning.
