Track 6present-tense revision

Revision of the present tense.

Track 7informal tu · this / these

With friends and family, use tu. Form it by adding -s to the “you / he / she / it” form.

falasyou speak
estásyou are
vendesyou sell
fazesyou do
este / estathis (m / f)
estes / estasthese
Track 8commands (imperative)

To give an order, switch tracks: -a verbs take -e, -e verbs take -a.

compre!buy!
venda!sell! (to o senhor)
vendam!sell! (to a group)

With tu, only negative commands switch tracks; positive ones just drop the s.

não vendas!don’t sell!
vende!sell!

On a positive command, hook the pronoun onto the end.

vendam-nas!sell them!
Track 9the “we” form

For “we”, drop the -r of the “to” form and add -mos.

falamoswe speak
comemoswe eat
saímoswe leave
vendemoswe sell
temoswe have
fazemoswe do / make

Before a pronoun, the -s drops and an l goes in: fazemo-lo = we do it.

Track 10ir is special · to arrive

ir bends the rules for “we” and “they”:

vamoswe go
vãothey / you go

chegadasarrivals

Hour 6

Irregulars, commands & reflexives

Track 1the -zer verbs (ço-go)

fazer, dizer and trazer change consonant in the “I” form — the “ço-go” verbs.

digoI say
tragoI bring (trazer = to bring)

Track 2-nho commands · avoiding commands

Verbs whose “I” form ends -nho take -nha as a command.

ponha!put!
venha!come!

To dodge the command form entirely, use pode + the “to” form.

Pode pô-lo aqui, por favor?Can you put it here, please?

The ço-go verbs swap -o for -a as a command (ça-ga).

Hour 7

Future, conditional & past

Track 1there is / are · we see each other
háthere is / there are
não há problemathere’s no problem
muita gentemany people
vemo-noswe see each other

This nos can mean “ourselves” or, with a sense of mutuality, “each other”.

Track 2the future — three ways

1) The present tense + a time word can carry the future.

2) ir + the “to” form = “going to”.

vou ligar-te esta noiteI’m going to call you tonight

3) The true future “will”: add -ei (I) or -emos (we) to the whole verb, stressing the ending.

falareiI will speak
comereiI will eat
começaremoswe will start
Track 3to wait / hope · the “sandwich”
esperarto wait / to hope
espero / esperareiI wait / I will wait

In the future, a pronoun gets sandwiched inside the verb.

comprá-lo-eiI will buy it

Sidestep the mouthful with “going to”:

vou comprá-loI’m going to buy it
Track 4remaining future endings

The rest of the future: -ás (tu), -á (you / he / she / it), -ão (you all / they).

Track 8European vs Brazilian Portuguese

This course teaches European Portuguese (EP). The main differences in Brazilian Portuguese (BP):

informal “you”EP tu (te)BP você
“I don’t understand you”EP não te compreendoBP não compreendo você
“what time do you get up?”EP a que horas te levantas?BP a que horas você se levanta?
“-ing” tenseEP estou a escreverBP estou escrevendo
“to put”EP pôrBP botar
“everyone”EP toda a genteBP todo o mundo

soundBP vowels sound more open; the final l and s, s before t, d and t before e / i, and the initial r are all pronounced differently.
