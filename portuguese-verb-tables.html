Present

what is / happens

Drop the -ar / -er / -ir, then add the ending. The stress sits on the second-to-last syllable.

-ar · falar-er · comer-ir · partir

Commands

do it!

Commands borrow the opposite track: -ar verbs take -e, the others take -a. The one exception is a positive order to tu, which just reuses the everyday ele / ela form.

-ar · falar-er · comer-ir · partir
você / o senhorone person, formalfale!coma!parta!
vocêsa groupfalem!comam!partam!
tu — positive“do it!”fala!come!parte!
tu — negative“don’t!”não fales!não comas!não partas!

Future

will

Add the ending to the whole verb (the infinitive). The endings are identical for every group — just hit the ending.

-ar · falar-er · comer-ir · partir
euIfalareicomereipartirei
tuyou · informalfalaráscomeráspartirás
ele / elahe / she / it · vocêfalarácomerápartirá
nóswefalaremoscomeremospartiremos
eles / elasthey · vocêsfalarãocomerãopartirão

Only the -zer trio is irregular here: they drop -zer → fazer · farei, dizer · direi, trazer · trarei. A pronoun gets sandwiched inside: comprá-lo-ei = “I will buy it”.

Conditional

would

Same idea as the future — built on the whole verb, one set of endings for all groups.

-ar · falar-er · comer-ir · partir
euIfalariacomeriapartiria
tuyou · informalfalariascomeriaspartirias
ele / elahe / she / it · vocêfalariacomeriapartiria
nóswefalaríamoscomeríamospartiríamos
eles / elasthey · vocêsfalariamcomeriampartiriam

The -zer verbs drop -zer here too: faria, diria, traria. The sandwich applies: fá-lo-ia = “I would do it”.

Past

did · finished

“Past is last” — hit the ending. Watch the I and the he/she/it rows; the others are tidy.

-ar · falar-er · comer-ir · partir
euIfaleicomiparti
tuyou · informalfalastecomestepartiste
ele / elahe / she / it · vocêfaloucomeupartiu
nóswefalámoscomemospartimos
eles / elasthey · vocêsfalaramcomerampartiram

The -ar “we” form is written with an accent (falámos) to set it apart from the present, though the course treats them as sounding alike.

These are the present-tense irregulars the course leans on. In the future and conditional, almost everything is regular (built on the infinitive) — only the -zer trio breaks the pattern.

One form covers “you / he / she / it” — o senhor, a senhora and você all ride along with the ele / ela row. tu is the separate informal “you”.
